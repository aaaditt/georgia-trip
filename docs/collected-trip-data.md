### 🏙️ Tbilisi — _City, views, culture_

Hot but dry at midday in August; lovely evenings. Plan = mornings + evenings out, pool/indoors midday. Almost everything is free.

**Family's rough group pick (informal, pre-vote note):** Cable car + funicular (kids + sunset), one private sulfur room (adults), museum for a hot afternoon. Rest is free.

**Online review sentiment (background research):** The cable car is rated a must-do for the views (note: the fortress interior is under renovation, but the trip up is still worth it). Mtatsminda is praised as the best city-lights/sunset spot — try the ponchiki donuts up top. The sulfur baths are called the "ultimate reset," though the scrub is intense and the baths aren't advised for pregnant women. Chronicles of Georgia is a quiet, dramatic, underrated favourite.

### 🍇 Kakheti — _Wine country_

Birthplace of wine (8,000 yrs), home of buried-clay-pot qvevri. Hot lowland — taste late-morning/late-afternoon, cellars are your cool refuge. Bases: pretty Sighnaghi / bigger Telavi (best wines nearby). Family cellars need a call ahead.

**Family's rough group pick (informal, pre-vote note):** Khareba cool tunnel (kid-friendly, mountain-cooled) + a view-lunch at Pheasant's Tears or a family cellar. Kids get juice + churchkhela.

**Online review sentiment (background research):** Visitors rave about the family-cellar hospitality — warm hosts, personal tours, "Georgian hospitality at its best," often with Caucasus/vineyard views. Pheasant's Tears is a natural-wine pilgrimage with a top restaurant next door. A few note the big-name wineries feel more commercial and rushed than the small maranis.

### 🏔️ Gudauri & Kazbegi — _Alpine, cool, glaciers (Marco Polo base)_

Your coolest, happiest zone. Mostly free nature; you pay for the 4×4 and thrill add-ons.

**Family's rough group pick (informal, pre-vote note):** 4×4 to Gergeti (all), easy Gveleti stroll, Friendship panorama — cool & light. Hiker adds Juta/Truso; the brave one does paragliding.

**Online review sentiment (background research):** Gergeti is called the "crown jewel of any trip" — majestic setting under Mt Kazbek (it's about the setting more than the church interior); the 4×4 road is rough but quick, vs a steep ~1–1.5 hr hike. Paragliding is a repeated "must-do," with travelers stressing how safe and professional it felt and how breathtaking the views are — but it's weather-dependent (can cancel), so keep it flexible, bring cash, and pick an experienced operator.

### 🌊 Kutaisi & Imereti — _Caves, canyons & water (the western leg)_

The best "fun adventure in pretty places without the heat." Caves stay cool, canyons are shaded & turquoise, mostly built paths. This is west — opposite direction from Kazbegi, so it means a bigger loop. Canyons/caves often close Mondays; Okatse closes in rain (Sataplia is the backup).

**Family's rough group pick (informal, pre-vote note):** Martvili boat + Prometheus cave — cool, shaded, both have a boat, thrill the kids, barely any walking. Add Okatse's walkway only if everyone's up for the ~2.5 km stroll (hiker's treat).

**Online review sentiment (background research):** Prometheus is called one of the most impressive stalactite caves people have seen (classical music inside; some find the optional boat short). Okatse's hanging walkway gets "Indiana Jones / breathtaking" reactions. Martvili's turquoise water is beautiful, though a few feel it's over-commercialised and the boat is short. Common tip: all three in one day needs a driver/logistics — hard to self-do.

### 🗼 Svaneti (Mestia & Ushguli) — _Medieval towers & big mountains (the road-trip add-on)_

The far-west highlands from the 13-day road-trip loop — medieval Svan tower villages under 5,000 m peaks. Kutaisi → Mestia is ~5 hr; Ushguli is a ~2 hr (each way) rough-road day trip. Cool in August, three nights recommended.

**Family's rough group pick (informal, pre-vote note):** Hatsvali cable car + Svan towers + kubdari lunch (easy for everyone); Chalaadi glacier walk for the hikers; full-day Ushguli for the Shkhara views.

**Online review sentiment (background research):** Travellers consistently call Svaneti the highlight of Georgia. Ushguli is one of Europe's highest continuously inhabited villages and UNESCO-listed; the Mestia–Ushguli road is rough but the scenery is unmatched. August is peak season — book guesthouses early.
